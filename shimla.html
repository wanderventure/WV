Story of Shimla

Most people know Shimla as a peaceful hill station, but very few realise that it actually served as the summer capital of British India and because of this, the British secretly built underground tunnels beneath the town. These tunnels were used to move important officials and confidential documents discreetly in case of unrest or attack. Some of these passageways are said to connect major heritage buildings like the Viceregal Lodge, Gaiety Theatre and Scandal Point, and although many of them are now sealed, locals still believe parts of the tunnel network exist below the city and remain unexplored even today.
